# Output: Yield Basis Leveraged BTC Liquidity Pulse

## What happened

Built an indexer for Yield Basis protocol — a leveraged BTC liquidity system built on top of Curve Finance by Michael Egorov.

## Angle

**Leveraged BTC Liquidity Pulse** — tracks the 2x leverage mechanism at the heart of Yield Basis. The protocol lets users deposit BTC (WBTC), borrows an equal amount of crvUSD from Curve, and pairs them in a Curve pool. The indexer captures:

1. **Vault deposits/withdrawals** — users entering/exiting yb-WBTC positions
2. **Borrower fee distributions** — the leverage cost flowing through the system
3. **Stablecoin allocations** — the protocol borrowing crvUSD to create leverage

## Key decisions

- Focused on the yb-WBTC LT (Leveraged Token) vault at `0x6095a220c5567360d459462a25b1ad5aead45204` — the most active Yield Basis market
- Used 2 tables instead of 4 from the scaffold: `vault_events` (deposits + withdrawals) and `leverage_ops` (fee distributions + stablecoin allocations) — cleaner and more focused
- Skipped AMM swap events (TokenExchange) since those happen in a separate Curve pool contract and would require additional address discovery; the vault events tell the complete leverage story

## Issues resolved

- Contract addresses not in contracts-registry — researched via Etherscan: found Factory at `0x370a449FeBb9411c95bf897021377fe0B7D100c0`, yb-WBTC LT vault at `0x6095a220c5567360d459462a25b1ad5aead45204`
- YieldBasisLT ABI was already generated by the scaffold (Vyper contract, verified on Etherscan)
- Portal cross-reference validation needed a sample-window approach (5000 blocks) instead of full-range query to avoid response truncation

## Data summary

- 7,862 vault events (4,671 deposits + 3,191 withdrawals)
- 7,724 leverage ops (7,721 fee distributions + 3 stablecoin allocations)
- 6 months of data: Sep 2025 → Mar 2026
- 4,000+ unique depositors
